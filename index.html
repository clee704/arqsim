Description

When a simulation starts, Sender transmits a packet to Receiver every time unit unless its window is full, i.e. there are W unacknowledged packets in its queue.

Following assumptions and simplifications are made in this simulation:

  • Packet length is constant and equal to one time unit.
  • Errors are independent and identically distributed.
  • The sequence number of a packet is always determinable even though it has errors.
  • Processing time is zero. For instance, as soon as the receiver receives a packet, it immediately sends back an ACK or NAK packet. The sender also operates this way.
  • ACK and NAK packet lengths are equal to zero and always sent error-free.
